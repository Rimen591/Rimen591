GT-APEX

Apex seals became a problem with the RX-8 and the turbo RX-7’s, as the higher temperatures, higher pressures and often insufficient lubrication by the factory oil metering pump (especially in the RX-8’s) to help meet emissions saw those later engines blow apex seals every 60,000-100,000 miles without additional maintenance and care.

Menzo

Yeah the first-gen RX-7’s are the exception to the rule. The 12A’s and early NA 13B’s were over-engineered and over-built for their power output, with thicker apex seals (3mm vs 2mm), and a more substantial oil metering pump (among other things) that saw the apex seals be much better lubricated, ensuring that both those engines could see mileages above 200,000 miles before blowing up with general, regular maintenance and proper care.
